# 01_Workflow_Map.md

The workflow has seven steps. Step 1: pick one buyer and one use case. Step 2: generate a printable concept using the prompt library. Step 3: create the page structure: cover idea, instructions, worksheets, trackers, and bonus page. Step 4: edit for clarity, originality, and usefulness. Step 5: format in Canva, Google Docs, PowerPoint, Affinity, or similar tools. Step 6: run the quality checklist for spelling, page size, margins, commercial claims, and duplicate content risk. Step 7: package the product with a listing title, description, mockup, delivery file, license note, and support FAQ. Repeat only after one product passes the launch audit.

# 03_Template_Guide.md

A strong printable template is simple, repeatable, and easy to understand. Recommended structure: Page 1 cover, Page 2 quick start guide, Pages 3-8 core worksheets, Pages 9-10 trackers or planners, Page 11 review page, Page 12 bonus checklist. Use consistent margins, font sizes, and spacing. Keep page titles action-based: “Plan Your Week,” “Track Your Tasks,” “Review Your Progress.” For KDP interiors, check trim size, bleed, page count, and Amazon KDP content rules before uploading. For PLR-style resale, create a clear license file and avoid using third-party graphics unless their license allows commercial redistribution. When unsure, replace assets with your own designs.

# 05_Quality_Checklist.md

Before selling, check every page manually. Does each page have a clear purpose? Can a buyer use it without extra explanation? Are fonts readable on letter and A4 sizes? Are margins safe for printing? Did you remove awkward AI phrases? Did you run spelling and grammar checks? Are all graphics either original, properly licensed, or from sources that allow commercial use? Are you avoiding fake income claims, medical claims, legal advice, or guaranteed outcomes? Is the product different from raw AI output? Does the download include instructions, license notes, and support details? If selling on WarriorPlus, do not claim approval, sales, buyers, or conversion data unless proven.
