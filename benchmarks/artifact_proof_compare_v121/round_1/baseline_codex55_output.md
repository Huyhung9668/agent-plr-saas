# 03_Template_Guide.md

Use a simple repeatable template structure. Cover page: product title, subtitle, intended user, and clean visual style. Instruction page: explain how to print, duplicate, or use digitally. Core worksheet pages: one task per page, clear heading, short instruction, enough writing space, and light visual elements. Tracker pages: include dates, checkboxes, notes, and progress reflection. Bonus page: add a quick reference checklist or mini planner. For KDP-style interiors, keep margins generous and avoid designs that rely on full bleed unless you understand print setup. For PLR-style resale, add editable source files, usage guide, and clear license terms written by you or reviewed professionally.

# 05_Quality_Checklist.md

Before selling, verify every page has a clear purpose and no filler. Check spelling, grammar, alignment, page numbers, margins, and export quality. Print at home or export to PDF and inspect readability at 100%. Confirm the product title does not use protected trademarks or celebrity names. Make sure AI-generated text has been edited and does not copy another product’s structure too closely. Confirm claims are practical, not guaranteed. Do not promise income, therapy results, academic outcomes, legal compliance, medical improvement, or platform approval. Buyer test: ask 5 target users if they understand the product, would use it, and what feels missing.

# 06_Fix_Prompts.md

Use these prompts when the first draft feels weak.  
Fix generic angle: “Narrow this printable idea to 5 specific buyer groups with urgent, repeated use cases. Avoid broad audiences.”  
Fix thin pages: “Improve this worksheet so the user completes a real task, not just reads advice. Add fields, checkboxes, prompts, and completion criteria.”  
Fix risky claims: “Rewrite this product description to remove income, health, legal, platform approval, and guaranteed outcome claims.”  
Fix AI sameness: “Make this printable feel more original by adding a unique workflow, sequence, naming system, and practical examples.”  
Fix refund risk: “Identify why buyers may feel disappointed and suggest improvements before launch.”
